Tutorial 4: Stereochemistry

This tutorial describes how to draw the following structure:

Drawing the Structure

To draw the structure:
1. Click the Single Bond tool.
2. In the document window, click and drag downward to create the first bond.
3. Point at the lower atom and click to add a second bond.
4. Continue pointing at the same atom and click again to add a third bond.
5. Point at the lower atom of the new bond to add a new bond.
6. Continue pointing at the same atom and click again to add the fifth bond.

 

Creating the initial structure

7. Point to the atom shown in the figure below and click twice to create two more bonds.

Repeating the bond sprouting

8. Now, turn the single bond at the upper right into a double bond using any of the methods described in the earlier tutorials.

 

Adding Atom Labels

1. Double-click the alpha carbon atom and type NH2 in the text box that appears, or press <N> on your keyboard. Press the <Esc> key when finished.
2. Add the O and OH with the text box. Optionally, you can press <O> on your keyboard.

 

Adding Stereochemical Properties

To add stereochemical properties to the structure, the carbon-nitrogen bond must be changed to a solid wedge bond.
1. To add the solid wedge, select the Wedged Bond tool, point to the carbon-nitrogen bond and click the bond.

 

Note: If you are drawing a structure from scratch, you can use the Wedged Bond Tool, , directly, instead of transforming any bond, for example, a single bond, to a wedged bond.
