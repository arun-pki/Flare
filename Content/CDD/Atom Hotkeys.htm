Atom Hotkeys

To apply atom hotkeys, place your mouse over an atom and press the appropriate key. For example, placing your mouse over an atom and pressing 1 will sprout a single bond in linear mode.

Sprout a carbonyl/acetyl group to the existing hotspot depending on its nature

On a primary carbon: adds an acetyl group to the hotspot
On a secondary carbon: a “C=O” appears on the hotspot
On a tertiary/aromatic carbon hotspot: adds a C-C bond and then an acetyl
 2

Sprouts a phenyl group to the hotspot

On a primary carbon hotspot: sprouts a phenyl group on the hotspot
On a secondary or tertiary/aromatic carbon hotspot: adds a C-C bond and then a phenyl group
 3 or a

Sprouts a wedged bonds to the atom hotspot

On a primary carbon hotspot: sprouts a wedged bond and a methyl group
On a secondary carbon hotspot: adds a wedged bond
On a tertiary carbon: adds a wedged bond in a vertical fashion
On an aromatic carbon hotspot: adds a C-C bond and then a wedged bond and another Me group
 4

Sprouts a hashed wedged bonds to the atom hotspot

On a primary carbon hotspot: sprouts a hashed wedged bond and a methyl group
On a secondary carbon hotspot: adds a hashed wedged bond
On a tertiary carbon: adds a hashed wedged bond in a vertical fashion
On an aromatic carbon hotspot: adds a C-C bond and then a hashed wedged bond and another Me group
 5

Sprouts a cyclohexane on the hotspot

On a primary carbon hotspot: sprouts a cyclohexane
On a secondary carbon hotspot (cyclohexane): adds a spiro cyclohexane
On a tertiary/aromatic carbon hotspot: adds a C-C bond and then a cyclohexane
 6

Sprouts a cyclopentane on the hotspot

On a primary carbon hotspot: sprouts a cyclopentane
On a secondary carbon hotspot (cyclohexane): adds a spiro cyclopentane
On a tertiary/aromatic carbon hotspot: adds a C-C bond and then a cyclopentane
 7

Sprouts a methylidene group on the hotspot

On any carbon hotspot: sprouts a methylidene group
On a tertiary or aromatic carbon, the valence is on purpose left wrong to accommodate for some specific heteroatom valences cases. Pressing [1] after will create a (E) double bond, pressing [0] after will create a (Z) double bond.
 8

Sprouts a dimethyl on the atom hotspot

On a primary carbon: adds a dimethyl group
On a secondary carbon: adds a gem-dimethyl group with correct angles
On a tertiary carbon: adds an isopropyl group
 9
Sprouts an alkyne group with the correct bond geometry z
