Tutorial 3: Perspective Drawings

In this tutorial, we explain how to create a perspective drawing by creating a model of a-D-glucose as a Haworth projection.

Draw a ring:
1. Click the Cyclohexane Ring tool.
2. Click in an empty area of canvas. A cyclohexane ring appears.
Rotate the ring:
1. Press <Ctrl>+ A(<Command>+A on macOS).
2. Drag the rotation handle clockwise, release the mouse button when the angle is about 30 degrees.

Change cyclohexane to tetrahydropyran:
1. Click outside the structure to deselect it.
2. Point to the atom in the figure below, and type the letter o.

Resize horizontally:
1. Press <Ctrl>+ A (<Command>+ A on macOS).
2. Using a selection tool, click-drag the right side handle to horizontally resize the ring. Release the mouse button when the ring is stretched about 200%.

Enlarge the ring:
1. Press <Ctrl>+A to select all.
2. Click-drag the ring corner adjustment handle to increase the size of the ring to 150%. Add vertical bonds:
3. Click the Solid Bond tool.
4. Point to the atom shown in the figure below and drag upward to create a bond.
5. Point to the same atom , and drag downward to create another bond.
6. Repeat this procedure four more times, adding the pairs of vertical bonds shown below:
Create OH labels:

For the Hotkey method, just point and type the letter o. The Text tool method is repeated here, with a slight variation.

1. Select the Solid Bond tool, point to the atom shown in the figure below, and double-click to open a text box.
2. Type OH.
Adding the OH labels
3. Move the pointer to the other atoms as shown, and triple-click to repeat the atom label.
Tip: If placing the labels is difficult because of the drawing size, go to View> Magnify to increase the size.
Add the CH2OH label:
1. Triple-click the upper atom of C5.
2. Press <Enter> to open the atom label text box. Type CH2 before the OH.

Change the type of the front bonds:

1. Click the Bold Bond tool.
2. Click the bond shown in the figure.
3. The cursor changes to a bold arrow as you point to the bond.
4. Click the Wedge bond tool.
5. Click each ring bond adjacent to the bold bond.
For each bonds, point slightly off center in the direction that you want the wide end of the wedge to be oriented and click.
Note: If the wedge is pointed in the wrong direction, click the bond again to flip its orientation.
